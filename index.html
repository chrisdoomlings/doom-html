What's different from the current page

Mockup note: any link or button that doesn't go anywhere in this file (review portal, social profiles, share buttons, "Browse all rewards") shows a hover tooltip describing what it would do in production. Hover over them to see the explanations.
  1. "Account created" is out of the earn grid — moved to the green "Welcome bonus earned" banner at the top. It's already done by default for every signed-in customer, so showing it as a tile alongside active CTAs was just noise.
  2. Modals replace page-jumps for one-tap claims. Social tiles, birthday, receipt, referral all open a modal — customer stays on /pages/rewards, taps once, sees ✓ Done, balance updates inline. Click any tile without the yellow "Opens portal" badge in this mockup to feel it.
  3. Review tiles still go to the portal (full page at /pages/write-a-review) — that's a rich, multi-step experience (OTC verify, product grid, file uploads) that doesn't fit in a modal. They carry an "Opens portal" badge to signal the page change.
  4. Birthday flow is now interactive. Customer enters DOB right in the modal; if today is their birthday, the 50 pts land immediately and the modal flips to a success screen.
  5. Referral modal includes a copy button + share buttons — Yotpo-style: paste your link, share to WhatsApp / Email / X without leaving the page.
  6. Social modals are slim and single-CTA: small icon, "+10 pts", one short line, one button — "Open Instagram ↗". The click is the claim — we grant the 10 pts when they tap the button, and the new tab opens to our profile. No "I followed" second step (it added friction and got skipped). One claim per platform, enforced server-side.
  7. Earn tiles are grouped: Shop & review · Refer · Follow us · Region-locked. Easier to scan than 14 identical squares.
  8. SMS tile is dimmed + region badge instead of being a live button this Pakistan-based customer can't claim.
  9. Birthday shown as "February 12", not 1986-02-12 — year is private.
  10. Order history moved to the bottom; the page is about points now.
